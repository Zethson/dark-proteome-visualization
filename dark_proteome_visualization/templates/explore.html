Scatter plot

Scatter plots are a simple way to visualize two numerical attributes of each point of a dataset. This type of visualization is well suited to reveal different types of correlations between the plotted attributes. Moreover, scatter plots of two different datasets (e.g. Eukaryotic proteins and Bacterial proteins) with the same visualized attributes, can be used to identify similarities and dissimilarities.

In this visualization, the darkness values is plotted against the attributes membrane, compositional bias or disorder. The data can be chosen from four different sub-datasets, which include proteins of eukarotic cells, bacterial cells, archaic cells or viruses. The visualization allows to explore the dataset by an integrated dragging and zooming function. Additional information about each data point can be obtained by hovering over the dot.

Parallel Coordinates

Parallel Coordinates provide a great way of visualizing correlations in high-dimensional spaces. Because of the size of the dataset that is visualized, individual lines are barely distinguishable. Therefore, the default opacity is set to 0.2, however you can adapt this to your needs. This enables to get a feeling for the distributions and the correlations of subsets of certain dimensions. For this visualization, the (maximum) number of samples used is limited, however you can increase this at your own risk and in terms of your capacities - we recommend to show up to 20000 samples. You can also use range-selection on the individual axes to only view the subset of samples that have the specified attribute within the selected range. You can move the selection-box and change it's size in either direction. To get rid of it, simply click on an unselected point on the axis.
